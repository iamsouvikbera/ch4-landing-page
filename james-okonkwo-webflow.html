the challenge

The GTM stack you'd never ship to production.

As a GTM Engineer, you've stitched it together yourself — and you know exactly where the seams are.

Five vendors and a duct-taped script

ZoomInfo for one field. Clearbit for another. Apollo as backup. A Python notebook that runs at 2am to glue them together. You built it; now you maintain it.

Every new play is a new BigQuery export

Marketing wants a new segment. Sales wants new triggers. Each one means another pipeline, another schema, another thing that breaks when someone changes a column.

The APIs you'd want don't exist

Or they do, with 100-call rate limits, no webhooks, and docs from 2019. Half your sprint is reading source to figure out what's actually available.

Reps want self-serve. You own the tooling.

Every "small change" routes through you. You're the bottleneck — and the team that thinks they want a no-code tool actually wants one with a real engineer behind it.
